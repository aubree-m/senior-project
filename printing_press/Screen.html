What is Screen Printing?

Screen printing is a versatile printing technique where ink is forced through a mesh screen onto a substrate. While traditionally using solvent-based inks, modern screen printing has evolved to use more environmentally friendly materials and processes.

The Screen Printing Process

  • A mesh screen is coated with light-sensitive emulsion
  • The design is transferred to the screen through exposure
  • Eco-friendly ink is pushed through the mesh onto the substrate
  • The print is dried using energy-efficient methods

Environmental Considerations

Traditional screen printing often used harmful chemicals and processes. Modern eco-friendly alternatives include:

  • Water-based and eco-friendly inks
  • Non-toxic cleaning solutions
  • Biodegradable emulsions
  • Recycled screen materials
  • Energy-efficient drying systems

Environmental Impact

Screen printing has historically been associated with chemical waste and VOC emissions. However, new technologies and materials have made it possible to create high-quality prints while minimizing environmental impact. Water-based inks, improved cleaning methods, and proper waste management have transformed screen printing into a more sustainable process.
