What is Letterpress Printing?

Letterpress printing is a traditional printing method where raised surfaces are inked and pressed into paper to create an impression. While historically significant, traditional letterpress printing often used harmful materials and processes. Modern letterpress printing has evolved to be more environmentally conscious.

The Letterpress Process

  • Type or image is set using raised surfaces
  • Eco-friendly ink is applied to the raised surfaces
  • Paper is pressed against the inked surfaces
  • The impression creates a distinctive tactile effect

Environmental Considerations

Traditional letterpress printing used lead type and petroleum-based inks. Modern eco-friendly alternatives include:

  • Soy-based and vegetable-based inks
  • Recycled and sustainable paper options
  • Photopolymer plates instead of lead type
  • Water-based cleaning solutions
  • Energy-efficient presses

Environmental Impact

Traditional letterpress printing used toxic materials like lead type and harsh cleaning solvents. Modern letterpress printers are adopting eco-friendly materials and processes to minimize environmental impact while maintaining the craft's distinctive quality.
